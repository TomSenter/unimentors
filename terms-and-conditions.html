2.0 Introduction
2.1 UNIMENTORS ltd is the owner of ‘www.unimentors.co.uk’ and allows for persons to sign up to either become a student or tutor or both. Although subject to change in the future, a student can only book an appointment with a tutor from their university and the reciprocal. A user can become both a tutor and a student using the same email address in the sign-up process - a user can then switch between their student and tutor accounts without having to enter different log-in details.

2.3 By registering on the unimentors website, a user agrees to and is bound by these terms and conditions, which are legally binding. Terms and conditions should be read in full by every user before agreeing to them.

5.0 Student Responsibilities and Rights
5.1 Students must be over the age of 18 to book a tutor
5.2 When students book a tutor, they have up to 10 hours before the booking to pay for the lesson before it is cancelled by unimentors.
5.3 Students must be truthful as to: whether a tutor is present to a meeting, whether a tutor is late, whether a tutor acts inappropriately i.e. offensively, illegally or offers plagiarism or collusion.
5.4 Students agree that if they wish to continue using unimentors, not to arrange bookings with tutors found using unimentors outside of the unimentors booking system.
5.5 When a student books a paid meeting with a tutor, the student enters a contract with the tutor that is not involved with unimentors and unimentors accepts no liability as to the tutor’s provision of lessons.
5.6 Students are to provide enough correct information to the tutor as to when and where they would like to meet for the tutor to know when and where they are meeting.
5.7 Students are responsible for checking that a tutor has the ability to teach them to a satisfactory standard based on the tutors profile and direct messages.
5.8 Students can cancel bookings for a full refund if they do so before 9 hours from the time of arranged meeting.

6.0 Tutors Responsibilities and Rights
6.1 Tutors must be at least 18 to sign up
6.2 Tutors must be legally entitled to be self-employed in the UK
6.3 Tutors are not employed by unimentors and are simply self-employed. Unimentors connects self-employed tutors to students.
6.4 Tutors are responsible for their own actions while dealing with a student in person or online or via the telephone.
6.5 Tutors must give unimentors notice of any previous or current criminal convictions.
6.6 Tutors must decide as to whether they can satisfactorily teach students based on what the students tell them that they need help with and their own knowledge and resources.
6.7 Tutors must not arrange meetings with students to educate them outside of the unimentors booking system. If they are found doing so, any account they hold with unimentors can be banned.
6.8 Tutors have the right to complain about offensive or illegal behaviour from students.
6.9 Tutors are required to report to unimentors of any requests of collusion or plagiarism from students either online or in person.

7.0 Complaints and Lesson Absences
7.1 Tutors and students must contact unimentors via email or telephone (provided on the contact us on the website footer) within 72 hours to state if the other person (tutor or student) has not showed to the meeting.
7.2 If ONE or BOTH of the two members of the meeting (tutor or student) claims that the other has not showed, then both are to present their evidence for unimentors to assess of which unimentors will use reasonable endevours to form a conclusion.
7.3 Students and tutors must submit complaints of behaviour directly to unimentors within a period of 96 hours from the time of tutor and student meeting.
7.4 Unimentors is to respond to complaints of behaviours and lesson absences within 96 hours of receiving the complaint.
7.5 If unimentors identify a sufficient complaint against a user (student or tutor), unimentors can ban the users account.

8.0 Payments
8.1 Tutors are entitled to 85-90% of the total paid by students, the rest is taken by Unimentors as charge for their service.
8.2 Students are to provide payment information before the booking is completed and their card is charged 12-24 hours after the meeting has ended
8.3 Payments may still be taken even if a student complains about a tutor or if the student reports the tutor not being present to the meeting. If Unimentors decides that complaint is sufficient or that the tutor was not present then the student will be refunded in full.
8.4 Students that cancel bookings over 10 hours before the booking do so with no charge. Unimentors allows tutors to decide if they wish to receive 40% of the total payment booking if the student cancels within 10 hours of the booking start time.
8.5 Tutors can cancel a booking at any point before the start time of a booking. They must inform the student directly and inform Unimentors of the cancellation also.
8.6 If a student payment does not go through and has not been paid before 48 hours after the end time of the lesson, Unimentors will cancel all future bookings that the student has made. Unimentors will attempt to retake the payment if not made after 24 hours of the original attempt
8.7 Unimentors may decide at their own discretion to pay the tutor for the full amount of the payment having not gone through, but are under no obligation to do so.
8.8 Unimentors is not liable for lost earnings of tutors as a result of student payments not going through.

The alterations will be accessible on the Unimentors website for 10 days after the change is made, after which point the Terms and Conditions will be available but not the changes.
